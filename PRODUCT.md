# Product

## Users

Book Time With serves solo service professionals: therapists, coaches,
consultants, contractors, tutors, and similar appointment-based businesses. A
small practice can use it only when one shared service, owner identity, and
calendar represent the whole booking page; staff scheduling and teams are not a
launch feature. Owners are usually configuring the product between client work
and need to publish or adjust availability quickly. Their clients usually arrive
from a text message, email signature, or social profile on a phone and need to
book without creating an account.

## Product Purpose

Give a service business one dependable booking link with the smallest useful
workflow: claim a handle, describe one service, paint working hours, and share
the page. Success means an owner can go live in under five minutes, manage the
business from one settings page, and trust that bookings, calendar state,
reminders, and client changes remain consistent without manual administration.

## Brand Personality

Calm, plainspoken, and quietly opinionated. The voice is warm but unsentimental,
confident without hype, and editorial rather than corporate. Copy should say
exactly what happens, acknowledge limitations honestly, and remove anxiety from
booking and schedule changes.

## Anti-references

- Bloated scheduling suites that turn a booking link into a CRM or workflow builder.
- Enterprise dashboards, feature grids, sales-led language, and configuration theatre.
- AI receptionist or automation hype that obscures the basic appointment workflow.
- Loud generic SaaS styling, decorative gradients, glass effects, and needless cards.
- Modal-heavy flows, hidden failure states, and interfaces that require training.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the promise: one link, one settings page, and no client account.
2. Make system state explicit: saving, sending, syncing, errors, and recovery are visible.
3. Protect trust before convenience: prevent conflicts, preserve privacy, and fail honestly.
4. Design phone-first for clients and interruption-first for busy owners.
5. Prefer a small complete feature over a broad feature with unfinished edges.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, semantics, focus, and keyboard operation. The
complete booking and manage flows must work by keyboard and screen reader.
Interactive targets should be at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels on touch surfaces,
essential meaning must never rely on faint color alone, inputs must avoid mobile
zoom, and motion must respect reduced-motion preferences. Timezone and scheduling
copy must be explicit for owners and clients in different regions.
